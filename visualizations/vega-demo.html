Exercise vs Sleep Quality

An interactive visualization exploring the relationship between weekly exercise hours and sleep quality ratings across different age groups.

What This Shows

This scatter plot visualizes sample data showing how regular exercise might correlate with sleep quality across three age groups. Each point represents a data observation, with:

  • X-axis: Weekly exercise hours (0-9 hours)
  • Y-axis: Sleep quality rating (1-10 scale)
  • Color: Age group (18-30, 31-50, 50+)

Key Insights:

  • Sleep quality tends to improve with moderate exercise (3-6 hours per week)
  • There appears to be an optimal range around 5-6 hours per week where sleep quality peaks
  • Excessive exercise (7+ hours/week) may correlate with slightly lower sleep quality
  • The pattern is relatively consistent across age groups

Note: This is sample data for demonstration purposes only and should not be used for medical or health decisions.
